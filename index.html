"Nonmeditation is to be free from the trap of meditating and not meditating. It means letting be in the state of naturalness, without focusing on any object or reference point, without trying to fabricate or alter anything, but simply allowing whatever occurs to arise and dissolve naturally. This is the essence of nonmeditation: to let everything be as it is, without trying to create a particular state of mind. The natural state is effortless and free from any need to alter or control. It is like the sky, open and vast, allowing clouds to come and go without any attachment or aversion. In this way, we recognize the true nature of our mind, beyond concepts and dualistic thinking. This is the practice of nonmeditation."
- Tulku Urgyen Rinpoche
